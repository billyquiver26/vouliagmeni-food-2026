Before you go
  • Both starred rooms are dark Sunday and Monday. Patio and Pelagos serve 7:00–10:30pm only, five nights a week. They are structurally occasion restaurants — neither can ever be the default. Book Pelagos weeks ahead.
  • Tasting menus need allergen notice at booking, not on arrival. Confirmed: no restrictions for either diner. If that ever changes, tell them when you reserve.
  • Greek meal times. Lunch runs 1:00–4:00pm, dinner rarely before 9:00pm. The magirio at O Charis is lunch-only and closes at 5:15pm sharp.
  • The 500-review floor was lowered to 150 for this guide. A resident can afford a miss; a tourist with three dinners cannot. Three of the best finds here have under 300 reviews.
  • Everything is tappable → opens in Google Maps, with Directions and Call on each card.

How these were chosen
  • Raw stars are bad evidence alone. 4.7 across 205 reviews is not the same claim as 4.7 across 4,914. Every rating below was pulled toward the dataset mean (4.494) in proportion to how thin its sample is: score = (v/(v+500))·R + (500/(v+500))·4.494.
  • Delta finished 20th. Patio finished 18th. Greece's only two-star restaurant and its Green Star neighbour both rank below a grill taverna in Varkiza. Both remain in the full guide below, stars intact.
  • Ratings measure satisfaction against expectation, not quality. A €40 Cretan taverna clears its bar more easily than a €200 tasting menu clears its own. This correction fixes sample size. It does not fix that.
  • Two entries here beat their score. Garbi and Ο ΧΑΡΗΣ are kept on function, not rank — one is the only room open twelve hours a day, the other the only magirio. Usefulness isn't in the arithmetic.

Pelagos ★
$$

One Michelin star, inside the Four Seasons Astir Palace. Chef Luca Piscazzi. Three tasting menus plus à la carte; the pomodoro and the pasta with caviar get named again and again. One diner who has eaten at both preferred it to Delta, Greece's only two-star. The stronger of the two starred rooms on the evidence.

The highest-rated room in this entire guide, Michelin included. Spaghetti alle vongole, pesto, prosciutto pizza. Complimentary bruschetta and focaccia to start. Portions are large enough to share — ask before you over-order. Closed Monday.

Central Athens

~25–30km · the full starred constellation · a few times a year
Michelin, Athens12 starred rooms exist in Greece — all of them are here. 2026 selection still pending.
Soil ★ 🌿
$$
★ 4.8 1,428 reviews
One star, one Green Star. Chef Mantis trained at Hof Van Cleve, Geranium and the Fat Duck. Tasting menu around €110; herbs and flowers come from their own ground. Spread over several floors past an open kitchen. Tables on the first floor sit close together. Closed Sunday.
Directions Call
CTC Urban Gastronomy ★
$$
★ 4.7 1,411 reviews
Twelve-course surprise menu with a Greek wine pairing, around €95 — the best value at this level in the city. Calamari "pasta", ceviche with kiwi. Service runs four and a half to five hours. Pacing has been criticised. Closed Sun + Mon.
Directions Call
Makris ★
$$
★ 4.6 551 reviews
Glass floor over Roman ruins in Thisio. The Utopia tasting menu; produce and herbs from chef Petros Dimas's family farm near ancient Corinth. Theatrical — dry ice, table poetry. Arrive willing to be entertained. The wine list draws complaints for its markup.
Directions Call
Spondi ★
$$
★ 4.5 1,419 reviews
Greece's first Michelin star, awarded 2002. In Pangrati, walking distance from Soil. French-leaning: foie gras with sorbet, monkfish, pigeon. A candle made of thyme-seasoned pork fat that you eat. Open seven nights, which none of the others manage.
Directions Call
Delta ★★ 🌿
$$
★ 4.4 677 reviews
The only two-star restaurant in Greece, at the Stavros Niarchos centre. Twelve courses of dishes disguised as other dishes; heavy fermentation. Chefs Papazacharias and Feskos came through Maaemo and Under. Note the dissent: some find the room feels like an office lobby in daylight, and at least one diner who has eaten both preferred Pelagos. Closed Mon + Tue.
